"W" Grade

You Should Know

The "W" (Withdrawal) grade is assigned when a student officially withdraws from a class between the 4th and final weeks of classes. The student must complete the withdrawal process by the withdrawal deadline, which is published on the BMCC Academic Calendar. "W" grades do not hurt your GPA, but they remain on your transcript without reason for withdrawal.

While GPA is not impacted by this grade, consider using "W" grades sparingly as multiple "W" grades might raise questions during transfer and/or grad school applications. Remember, there are deadlines to withdraw with a "W", missing them could result in a failing grade. Additionally, even though "W" grades won't affect gpa, they can delay graduation and impact financial aid. Before withdrawing, consider discussing your situation with your professor. They can offer alternative solutions like tutoring or changing sections and help you understand the implications of withdrawing.

Get In Touch

If you are a recipient of a "W" grade, or are expecting to get one, and have any questions or concerns, contact StudentSuccess@bmcc.cuny.edu. If you are a member of ASAP, BLA, College Discovery, or CUNY EDGE, you may choose to contact your advisor.
