"NC" Grade

You Should Know

The "NC" (No Credit) grade is assigned when eligible students apply for the grade and are approved. It is an option for freshmen (with no more than 30 credits) and nursing students (2nd-4th semesters) under specific conditions. You can apply for NC in courses with a failing grade (C- or below) but must still attend class, complete assignments, and take the exam. There is a deadline to opt-in for this grade, which can be found on the BMCC Academic Calendar. A maximum of 8 credits, or 2 classes, can qualify for this grade.

"NC" grades do not hurt your GPA, but they remain on your transcript. While GPA is not impacted by this grade, consider using "NC" grades sparingly as multiple "NC" grades might raise questions during transfer and/or grad school applications. In some cases, other schools may even consider an "NC" grade to be equivalent to an "F" grade. Additionally, even though "NC" grades won't affect gpa, they can delay graduation and impact financial aid.

Get In Touch

If you are a recipient of an "NC" grade, or are expecting to get one, and have any questions or concerns, contact StudentSuccess@bmcc.cuny.edu. If you are a member of ASAP, BLA, College Discovery, or CUNY EDGE, you may choose to contact your advisor. If you would like to get in a touch with a relevant department, you may refer to the Contacts page for more information.
